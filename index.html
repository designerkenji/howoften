How It Works
Log once. Understand everything.
⚡

One-Tap Logging

Record any event in under a second. No forms, no friction. The faster you can log, the more complete your data becomes.

📈

Pattern Recognition

How Often automatically surfaces trends — time-of-day clusters, day-of-week spikes, correlations between different events you track.

💡

Meaningful Insights

Not just charts. Plain-language observations that answer the real question: is this getting better, worse, or staying the same?

🔒

Private by Design

Your data stays on your device. Health and personal patterns are sensitive — we built How Often with that in mind from day one.

📋

Doctor-Ready Reports

When you need to share, export a clean timeline. Arrive at appointments with actual data instead of "I think it's been more frequent lately."

🗓️

Time-Based Context

Compare this week vs. last month. See how frequency changes after you start or stop something. Time is the lens that makes data meaningful.

Real-World Uses
What are you trying to understand?
Health
"Is my coughing actually getting worse?"
Track symptoms over weeks, not just gut feelings. Spot whether episodes cluster after certain foods, weather, or activities.
Habits
"How often am I actually exercising?"
The truth about your habits, with no judgment. See streaks, gaps, and whether your frequency is trending the direction you want.
Parenting
"Is my child's tantrum frequency changing?"
Log incidents and let patterns emerge. Identify triggers by time of day, activities, or routines you've recently changed.
Well-being
"What's causing my anxiety spikes?"
Track mood events alongside daily variables. Over time, correlations surface that you'd never notice in the moment.
Pets
"Are these episodes more frequent this month?"
Pets can't tell you if something's getting worse. Your log can. Arrive at vet appointments with a precise frequency history.
Anything
"How often does this actually happen?"
Memory is a terrible frequency counter. How Often isn't. If you've ever wondered "is this more often than before?" — this is your app.
